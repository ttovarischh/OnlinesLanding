Basics of design taught by practitioners, industry experts

Communication designer is responsible for all visual communications of a product or a brand

He solves its problems through design. Creates an identity which is appropriate to the brand’s or product’s objectives and audience. Then distributes it by all means of customer communication: via packaging, website, and corporate products. On merchandise and souvenirs.

Anyone can become a communication designer — with practice and advice. We will start from the visual identity, then gradually proceed to more complex tasks: digital, graphic, and typographic.

You will

create your brand’s visual identity

develop and animate graphic and typographic posters

use collages to visualise space, interior, or landscape of your project

design a book, a guide, a catalogue, or a magazine. Typography, layout, pre-press

study your target audience, analyse competitors

work in Adobe Illustrator, Photoshop, Indesign, After Effects, Premiere

You can become an

  • art-director
  • infography & branding specialist
  • motion, media, or book designer
  • fonts, packaging, or ad designer
  • graphic designer
  • web designer
  • pre-press specialist

Course participants study in a convenient digital environment

Clear theory and relevant data

We follow the design agenda, update and supplement the materials. Each module is a balance between practical and theoretical skills. You work with design software, get to know artistic practices, and receive a theoretical base.

Face-to-face, practical, real-time learning

Students meet 3 times a week in a digital campus: receive educational materials and guidelines. It is not a pre-recorded course, but practice in small groups under the guidance of a curator. 1 group — up to 24 people.

Project portfolio

There are 4 modules in this course. Within each module students prepare and defend a project. Projects vary in difficulty — from graphic and typographic posters to a multi page publication: a book, a magazine, or a catalogue. They help to track your progress, become a tool for promotion and recruitment.

FAQ

Is communication design the right profession for me?

Is communication design the right profession for me?

If you become a communication designer, you are free to choose a preferable area. You can layout books and magazines, work in branding or advertising. Any field in visual art and design is open if you possess the necessary skill set and a portfolio.

Is it possible to become a designer in a year?

Is it possible to become a designer in a year?

We don't ever stop learning. During the first year you will have acquired basic professional skills to start earning a living in your new profession.

What are the entry requirements?

What are the entry requirements?

Be curious, motivated, and interested in the field of design and visual arts. We do not have any professional requirements for entrants to the programme.

How and when will I learn?

How and when will I learn?

You will study on a timetable face-to-face with your tutor in real time. It is not a pre-made course, but practice under the guidance of group teachers.

Who's going to teach me?

Who's going to teach me?

Current practitioners with a track of professional experience and a portfolio of completed projects. Design is a practical discipline, and should be taught by people who work in the creative industry.

What should I do if I can't cope with the workload?

What should I do if I can't cope with the workload?

Our teachers are always ready to help and advise you in case of problems. If you are interested in learning a new profession, you are sure to do well. Design of this programme ensures it.

What if I can't draw and am not creative enough?

What if I can't draw and am not creative enough?

ONLINES courses do not have entry requirements. Programme tutors will help you to learn all basic creative skills, get to know the genres of communication design.

Will I be able to find a job after this course?

Will I be able to find a job after this course?

Communication design is a cross-border industry. The knowledge and skills you acquire, as well as your portfolio of completed projects, will help you to compete for clients anywhere in the world.
